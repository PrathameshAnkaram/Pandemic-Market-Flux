# Pandemic Market Flux: Analyzing COVID-19's Impact on Investor Behavior and Stock Performance

## Overview
This project analyzes the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on investor behavior and stock performance, focusing on three major stocks listed on the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE): HDFC, Infosys, and Reliance. The goal is to understand how different sectors responded to the pandemic-induced market volatility and to provide strategic recommendations for investors and policymakers.

## Data Sources
- **Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE)**: Historical stock data for HDFC, Infosys, and Reliance.
- **Economic Indicators**: Additional data used includes key economic indicators relevant to the market conditions during the pandemic.

## Tools Used
- **SQL**: For data extraction and initial data handling.
- **Python**: Comprehensive data analysis, including:
  - Pandas and NumPy for data manipulation.
  - Matplotlib and Seaborn for data visualization.
  - Scikit-learn for predictive modeling.


## Analysis Summary
- **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**: Visualized trends in stock prices and trading volumes.
- **Predictive Modeling**: Applied linear regression to forecast future stock performance.
- **Sector Analysis**: Evaluated sector resilience during the pandemic.

## Key Findings
- The technology sector (Infosys) showed strong resilience due to increased demand for digital services.
- Financial services (HDFC) and energy sectors (Reliance) experienced significant challenges but recovered due to adaptive strategies and market corrections.

## Recommendations
- **Diversification**: Investors should diversify their portfolios to mitigate risks associated with market volatility.
- **Sector Focus**: Technology stocks offer potential growth stability during economic downturns.

## Conclusion
The project provides insights into the dynamic nature of stock markets in response to global crises and offers actionable strategies to navigate future market uncertainties.
